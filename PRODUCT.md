# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed: data professionals/analysts who generate diagrams frequently as part of reporting workflows, and generalist knowledge workers (PMs, founders, marketers) who drop in occasionally to turn a quick idea into a diagram without learning a tool. The dashboard has to stay dense and fast for the former without intimidating the latter. Public-facing surfaces (landing, share links) are often a casual user's first and only touchpoint.

## Product Purpose

VisualMe turns a plain-English description into one of 19 diagram types automatically, lets users edit and iterate on the result, then save, share, or export it. Success is a user going from "I have an idea in my head" to "I have a diagram I can put in a deck" in under a minute, and trusting the tool enough to come back for the next one.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, expressive, distinctive. Since the product's whole value is turning information into considered visual form, the interface itself should demonstrate that same visual confidence and point of view, not hide behind a generic dark-SaaS shell. Voice is direct and unpretentious: it shows the result, it doesn't oversell it.

## Anti-references

The current build: slate-900 background, #6366f1 indigo accent, glass-panel blur cards, gradient buttons, Space Grotesk on everything. This is the default "AI startup" template look, indistinguishable from a thousand other dark-mode SaaS products, and the execution is inconsistent across pages (uneven spacing, hierarchy, contrast). Avoid anything that reads as a Tailwind-UI-kit-out-of-the-box assembly: identical card grids, hero-metric blocks, glassmorphism as decoration, gradient text, side-stripe accents.

## Design Principles

- **The product proves the pitch**: a visualization tool whose own UI has no visual point of view is not credible. Every screen should look like it was designed by people who care about how information is presented.
- **Density without intimidation**: serve the power user's need for speed and information density (Figma-grade execution rigor: deliberate spacing, weight, alignment, hierarchy) while keeping entry points calm enough for a first-time visitor.
- **Consistency over novelty per-screen**: fix the actual craft problem first, rigorous and repeatable spacing/type/color decisions across dashboard, saved, settings, and landing, rather than reskinning each page with a different idea.
- **Show, don't sell**: let generated diagrams and real product moments carry the visual weight, especially on the landing page; minimize generic marketing scaffolding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No special requirements called out for this pass; standard good practice (sufficient contrast, keyboard reachability, respecting reduced-motion) is enough, not a blocker for shipping design changes.
